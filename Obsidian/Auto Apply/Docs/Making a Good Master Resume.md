# A detailed Resume is a Good Resume
The single best thing you can do to improve Auto Apply's output is give it a strong, detailed profile to work from. Every résumé and cover letter is tailored *down* from what's in your profile — the AI can rephrase, reorder, and emphasize what you give it, but it can't invent experience you never entered. A thin profile produces thin, generic documents. A rich one gives the AI real material to match against each job.

The fastest way to start is to upload your master résumé during setup so it's parsed into structured fields. But the parsed result is a starting point, not the finish line. Treat your profile as a **master résumé** — a superset of everything you've ever done — and keep expanding it over time. A per-job résumé is a one-page cut of it; your profile itself should be as long and complete as you like.

# What makes a profile strong

## Be specific and quantify
Vague claims give the AI nothing to tailor. Concrete, measurable ones do.

- ❌ "Responsible for improving system performance."
- ✅ "Cut API p95 latency from 800ms to 120ms by adding Redis caching and batching DB queries, serving 2M requests/day."

Numbers, tools, and outcomes are what job descriptions ask for — put them in.

## Name your technologies explicitly
Scoring and skill-matching work by comparing the exact skills in a job description against the exact skills in your profile. If a job wants "PostgreSQL" and your profile only says "databases," that's a gap the AI sees. List concrete tools, languages, frameworks, and platforms by name (Python, FastAPI, Docker, AWS, React…), on the relevant experience and project items — not just in a skills list.

## Write for each item, not just the header
For every job, project, or piece of education, capture:
- **What you actually did** — the concrete work, not the job title's implied duties.
- **What you were responsible for** — scope, ownership, who/what you led.
- **What technologies you used** — named, per the point above.
- **What the outcome was** — the measurable result, ideally quantified.

The more of this the profile holds, the more the AI has to choose from when tailoring to a specific posting.

## Include more than you'd put on one résumé
Old jobs, side projects, coursework, one-off contributions — put them all in. Auto Apply picks what's relevant to each job and leaves the rest out. Something irrelevant to today's application may be exactly what tomorrow's posting is looking for. You lose nothing by having it in the profile; you lose opportunities by leaving it out.

## Be honest
The AI presents your profile persuasively, but it works from what you enter. Don't inflate titles or claim tools you can't speak to in an interview — a tailored document that gets you a screen you then fail helps no one. Enter what's true, and let the tailoring make the best honest case for it.

# Use score breakdowns to find the gaps
When you score a job, read the breakdown carefully. It tells you which skills and requirements from the posting your profile matches and which it's missing. Recurring gaps across many jobs are a signal: either it's a genuine skill to develop, or it's something you *do* have but never wrote down. If it's the latter, go add it to your profile — that's the cheapest résumé improvement there is.

Over time, scoring a range of jobs in your target role shows you the shape of what employers want, and points you straight at what to flesh out next.
